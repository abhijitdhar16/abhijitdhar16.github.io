CCCP or USSR

The Soviet Union, officially the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR), was a federal socialist state in Northern Eurasia that existed from 1922 to 1991. Normally it was a union of multiple state but in practice it's economy and government were highly centralized until it final years. All USSR stamps are printed in the name CCCP which is Soviet abbreviation of USSR.

Bahawalpur

Bahawalpur was a princely state of British India, and later Pakistan, that existed as an independent nation from 1924 to 1955. It was a part of Punjab States Agency. The state had a population of around 1,341,209 in 1941. The capital of the state was the town of Bahawalpur. Currently it belongs to Panjab province of Pakistan.

British India

The British India was the rule by the British Crown on the Indian subcontinent from 1858 to 1947. The rule is also called Crown rule in India, or direct rule in India. The region under British control was commonly called India in contemporaneous usage, and included areas directly administered by the United Kingdom, which were collectively called British India, and areas ruled by indigenous rulers, but under British tutelage or paramountcy, called the princely states. The region as a whole was never officially referred to as the Indian Empire.
