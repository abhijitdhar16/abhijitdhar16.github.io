Azad Hind

The name Azad Hind means independent India. INA (Indian Nation Army) was an armed force formed by Indian nationalists in 1942 in Southeast Asia during World War II with the help of Japan and Germany. Their aim was to azad (independent or free) India from British rule. These stamps were printed in the Nazi Germany in the name of new country to be born. INA was defeated and these stamps were never used.


British Guiana

British Guiana was a former British colony, part of the British West Indies, which resided on the northern coast of South America, now known as the independent nation of Guyana. The first European to encounter Guiana was Sir Walter Raleigh, an English explorer.

Mahara state

The Mahra Sultanate, in full Mahra Sultanate Of Qishn And Socotra, was semi-independent state in the southern Arabian Peninsula, including the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean. This is in now eastern Yemen. In 1886-1888, the Sultanate signed a British protectorate treaty, accepting British protection for his lands. Then later country is merged to the Aden Protectorate. The last sultan of Mahra was deposed in 1967-68, when the British vacated the region. The functional government of Mahra State printed many beautiful stamps like this on various themes. They are mainly issued for the interests of the philatelists and fund-rising. Many of these were never used for postal purpose.
